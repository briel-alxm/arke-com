§ 03 Features & Capabilities

Features & Capabilities.

Six fields per deal: Deal ID (auto), Customer Name, Opportunity, Deal Value, Deal Stage (Registered / Qualified / Quoted / Won), and Submission Date. Across four pipeline views: Pipeline, Deal List, Dashboard, Calendar. Full breakdown on the Pilot page.

In the pilot, no — the six fields are deliberately fixed, because the constraint is part of what makes the pilot diagnostic. In commercial deployment (Premium), yes — field configuration is part of how we tailor the system to your industry. See Advanced for the full set.

Premium deployment includes integrations with the tools most hardware teams already use — email, Slack, QuickBooks, common accounting systems. If you use something specific, mention it in the pilot request and we'll tell you honestly whether it's in scope.

§ 04 Commercial Deployment

Commercial Deployment.

A thirty-minute conversation about what worked. From there: Standard deployment, Premium deployment, another pilot round if the situation calls for it, or we part well. The decision is yours, not ours.

Standard is $3,500 one-time. Premium is $5,000 one-time. Both are per deployment — no per-seat fees, no subscriptions required. Optional ongoing support is $300 – $500 / month, cancellable any month. Full breakdown on Pricing.

Standard includes thirty days post-launch; Premium includes sixty. After that, ongoing support is optional — most teams don't need it once the system is bedded in. If you do, the subscription tier covers tuning, quarterly architecture reviews, and priority issue response.

Standard: two to three weeks from kickoff to live system. Premium: four to six weeks, depending on integration scope. Because the pilot gives us a calibrated starting point, deployment moves faster than it would from cold.

§ 05 Technical & Data

Technical & Data.

Your data lives in your own Airtable workspace — not on a Briel server. You control access, permissions, and audit logs through Airtable directly. We never read or store your deal data on our infrastructure.

Because the system runs on Airtable, uptime is Airtable's published SLA — historically above 99.9%. We don't add a layer that could fail; we configure the layer that's already proven.

Any time — CSV, JSON, or direct API access. Because it's your Airtable workspace, the data is yours by default. We don't lock you in; if you ever leave, you walk away with everything intact.

The architecture is platform-aware, not platform-locked. If Airtable becomes the wrong substrate, we have a migration path — the field schema, workflow logic, and integrations are documented in a portable spec. You'd never have to rebuild from zero.
