§ 03 Pricing Questions

The questions we hear. And honest answers.

Because the value is in the architecture, not the access. Once your system is deployed, your team owns it. You don't pay rent on infrastructure that's already working. Ongoing support exists for the teams that want it — never as a gate.

Premium includes field configuration tuned to your industry, vendor categories, deal stages renamed to your vocabulary, and integrations with tools your team already uses (most common: email, Slack, QuickBooks, accounting systems). Standard ships configured as we recommend; Premium ships configured as you operate.

The list price is the price. We hold it because it's calibrated to the work — not against a discount we're hoping you'll ask for. If your situation is unusual (multi-team rollout, a deep integration project, or you're an early reference partner), we'll talk scope honestly.

You can upgrade to Premium at any point for the difference ($1,500) plus the configuration scope. We don't penalise the choice to start narrow — most teams should. The training path shows when each advanced feature usually earns its place.

The pilot is free, and it stands on its own. If you choose to deploy after the pilot, you pay the Standard or Premium fee in full — but the work done during the pilot informs how we configure your deployment, so nothing is lost.
