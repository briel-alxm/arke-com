§ 01 Why Later, Not Now

Power is layered in. Not bolted on.

The pilot ships with six fields, four stages, and one dashboard — on purpose. Advanced features are designed against real operating need, not anticipated complexity. We introduce each one only when your team has earned the right question: "what would help here?"

Everything below is available. None of it is required.

§ 02 The Features

Four capabilities. Each load-bearing.

◆ Feature 01

Vendor Management.

Treat each vendor relationship as a first-class object — not a column in a spreadsheet. Track terms, contacts, escalation paths, and history. When a deal touches vendor capacity, the constraint is visible before it becomes a problem.

Use case
Two vendors quote the same component. Which has the better track record on lead time? The system has the answer before you ask.
◆ Feature 02

Deal Analytics.

Beyond the dashboard — sizing tiers, strength ratings, win-rate by stage, time-in-stage distribution. The signal that lets you say "this quarter's pipeline is healthier than last quarter's" and mean it.

Use case
Your win-rate at "Quoted" dropped from 60% to 38% over six weeks. Analytics surfaces it; you decide whether to investigate, recalibrate, or stand pat.
◆ Feature 03

Automated Workflows.

Auto-escalations when a deal stalls. Notifications when a quote crosses a threshold. Reminders before a follow-up window closes. The routine work the system handles so the operator doesn't have to.

Use case
A $40K deal hasn't moved in fourteen days. The system flags it to the deal owner — and, if it idles another seven, to their manager. Quietly. Reliably.
◆ Feature 04

Risk Assessment.

Every deal carries a signal score — a composite of stage age, value, customer history, and engagement signals. Not a verdict; a flag. The score answers "where should I look first this morning?"

Use case
Twelve deals are open. Three carry warning scores. You review those three before checking email. The quarter shifts by the order in which you spend the first hour.

§ 03 The Training Path

When each feature gets introduced. And why.

Sequence matters. Introducing risk scores before the team can read the dashboard is noise pretending to be sophistication. We follow this order with every client.

Weeks 01 – 04 · Pilot
Core pipeline only — six fields, four stages, one dashboard. Build the muscle for the substrate first.
Month 02 – 03
Vendor Management — once the team has logged real deals and the relationships matter.
Month 03 – 04
Deal Analytics — once there is enough data to read meaningful patterns, not noise.
Month 04 – 06
Automated Workflows — once you know which manual steps are actually expensive.
Month 06+
Risk Assessment — the last to arrive, because it depends on every layer beneath it.

This sequence is a default, not a rule. Some teams need analytics earlier, some delay automation. We adjust against the actual shape of your pipeline.
